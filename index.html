What is neuromorphic computing?

Neuromorphic computing, also known as neuromorphic engineering, is an emerging technology inspired by the human brain.
It is meant to replicate the structure and function of a human brain.
Unlike more traditional computer engineering, it draws from biology and neuroscience.
Conventional processors rely on sequential, high-power computation; Neuromorphic systems are meant to process similarly to neurons and synapses, event-driven, parallel, and adaptive.
These euromorphic computers are meant to be extremely energy efficient, have real-time decision-making, greater adaptability and machine learning, and better parallel processing or multitasking.
